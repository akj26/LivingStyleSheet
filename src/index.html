DID(it) Style and Branding Guidelines

DID(it) (Digital Innovation & Development (Information Technology)) is an innovative technology and development group within the U.S. Citizenship & Immigration Services division of DHS. DID(it) uses innovative technology and development processes which produces rapid and responsive web applications in order to enhance USCIS business operations and support the mission of USCIS and DHS.

This living style sheet highlights guidelines and information for using the DID(it) branding for both print and web: including the logo, colors, font and images for both print and web.

Our message and visual imagery communicates the message of our mission: professional, innovative, agile and responsive, and these guidelines give a clear direction in how this style and branding can be used to communicate consistent and innovative messages

This style guide offers the foundation of the DID(it) branding including distinctive color palettes, typefaces, standardized uses of imagery, and a grid system that helps to make layout more clean and open. This set of guidelines are to be considered just that, guidelines, and we trust that room has been left for creativity as long as it’s is consistent with the DID(it) message laid out in this document.

The reason it’s important to leave room for creativity and expansion for those designers and employees of USCIS-DHS that will use this guide is because the DID(it) philosophy is to bravely approach all existing elements of technology in all its forms not be afraid to color out of the box.
